Coding BBQ is <u>yet another honest attempt</u> to log my thoughts online. I was active online earlier but that is a story for another time.

Why this blog?
1. To Improve my thought process.
I have been in the Software Industry for around 9+ years now but have seen exponential technology changes and not to mention the competition. The best way to remember
a concept is when you can teach that. I strongly believe writing my thoughts will help me improve in a lot of areas.

2. To keep me accountable.
Now that I have this journal, my concious will always motivate me to learn new things and keep posting my learnings in a very concise and straightforward manner. 
Not only will I have to learn new concepts, keep practicing my programming skills, but with this weblog, it will also help me think straight.

3. Share my knowledge.
and have my own knowledge repository and a time cube which I can revisit. I wanted to have a place where I could document my day-to-day programming experiances. Apart from that, also writing technical blog posts will help me generalize the overall problem for anyone else's consumption. Writing will also help me improve my verbal as well as written communication skills.

Why Jekyll?
1. Technical blog
Jekyll, has its major advantages. It is easy to setup. However, my main objective with this blog is to improve my technical skill and that's where Jekyll comes into picture. Github pages support jekyll for hosting content and this means I will have liberty of how the blog will appear. Not only will I learn a little bit of Ruby, Sass and more of markdown, It will help quickly publish my thoughts while in my editor of choice.

2. Practice programming
I wanted to post code snippets online and jekyll has great support for that. This means, I will be accountable of learning new concenpts regularly. Previously, on blogspot, I had hard time posting code snippets.


I hope to keep BBQ'ing often and cook good programming recepies. See you around!!!
