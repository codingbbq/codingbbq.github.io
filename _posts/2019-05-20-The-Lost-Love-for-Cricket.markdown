The year 2019 is a great year for cricket fanatics. We had IPL for the past couple of months and now the fun will continue in the World Cup. Cricket in the news, the social media, advertisements, coffee breaks, just everywhere. I, for one, have no interest whatsoever in this sport. I have not watched a single IPL match since its inception around a decade ago. I literally unfollowed the sport.

Growing up, it was different. I loved to play cricket. We played almost every single day after school. We lived in a peaceful, off main road lane where the traffic was negligible. However we did not have playground, and no one could refrain us from playing, so we would have our mini playground wherever we found an empty spot. Inside our house on the balcony, or in one of the rooms, on the road (literally in the middle of the road), on the pavements besides the closed shops (usually on a Sunday). On some decided occasions, we would walk to a playground nearby or cycle to a beach. Wherever the guys gathered, it was to play Cricket. 

Here is a pic of a ball my parents purchased for me. At the time, it was way too expensive. I was the happiest excited top of the world kid to receive such a precious gift. Couple of days back I found it hidden in a long forgotten treasure of my childhood, it brought a smile on my face. It is still shiny little round ball with amazing memories. But, just like the forgotten ball, I too have lost the love for the sport. 

![The Long Lost Love for Cricket](/assets/images/The-Lost-Love-For-Cricket.jpg){:class="img-fluid w-100"}

The transition for my love for the sport to the point where I completely ignore and filter out all stuff Cricket was gradual. Growing up, I had kept a record of all the players playing the world cup from each and every team. For that, I had to watch the entire match because only then I would see the names of the players on my television screen. We did not have internet back then and I did not like reading newspaper. So yes, I watched a lot of sport. 

I remember, in my Higher secondary education, the chemistry tutor said one very important thing. He said, "I would encourage you all to play 4hrs of cricket, than watch half hour of it on the television". This struck me and ever since I have realised, that Cricket is not the entertainment choice I love. For everyone I debate about this choice, the only argument is that it is so much fun to watch a teeth biting match, it is pure entertainment. I choose to disagree and walk away silently without arguing. 

This post is not about why you should play cricket and stop wasting/utilising your time in watching an IPL or a World cup. No. I write about cricket because of the treasure I found which my parents had gifted me. Which brings me to the fact that how much I cherished that cricket ball, I never let it touch the ground. For the fear it would dirty or break. I wanted to keep it as it is, and so, I kept it inside a Christmas sock. I was so possessive about it, I would not share it with anyone. During the rainy days, I would sit holding it tight praying to the Sun to drive away the watery clouds. Nostalgic. 

I have the best childhood memories of me playing cricket, I savour them. These are the memories written on the rock and will not easily fade away.  Growing up, my priorities changed, so did my interest and I definitely considered watching cricket as one of the biggest waste of Time. I unfollowed it. I have no regrets.
