Fresh out of college, I had no clue what websites are and what website designing means? I only knew that we could create exciting things online. 

Internet was the new thing for me, when I was studying. I have lived to the days of dial-up connections. I remember standing in the queue of my ISP and handing over some money to get my dial-up recharged. I remember signing up on yahoo and sending forwards to my friends. Every new email got itself some excitement. 

In this post, I visit my internet days and how I started blogging/web designing etc. 

### Blogger

I started writing on blogger. It was free and I could interact online and share my thoughts. I wrote about various things like tutorials, Gmail, google reader etc. The best part about Internet then was it was the new thing. I had just got myself broadband connection and that is when I spent most of my time online. 

I occasionally played counter strike but most of the time, I was exploring blogging. I learned about Php and back then it was the easiest thing to learn and had the least turnaround time to completion of project. I was a big fan of Envato and having read thousands of articles on their tutorials page, I learned about freelancing and creating websites.

### Freelancer ?

I took up a freelancing gig to get a website done for a business that was creating signage, awards and trophies. The idea was to make them a good looking website. Back then I accidentally stumbled upon creating and sending out HTML emails. I had no clue what the end result was out of this efforts seating in front of a PC, however my reading and constant research gave me a boost and added to my experience. I remember creating good looking HTML emails and sending them out one by one to almost 300 plus email ID's. A donkey job, but yes, I had done it.  

### Website designer ?

Coming back to website design. I had zero experience in website design. However I did a lot of reading and took a lot of references from different free to use websites etc.  To me, the true purpose of photoshop software was to design websites. So I got my hands dirty using photoshop all the time. I created some basic design templates which were then to be converted to HTML. 

If I were to be a one man software developer, I had to learn how to design. Or how would I create a website? There was no Bootstrap back then. 

The journey was amazing, however I did not finish the project.

The current situation is that, photoshop is for photo retouching, and we have dedicated designers who are usually called as UX or User Experience professionals who do the designing and creating of screens for the web developers to consume.

### Takeaway

I was happy to find these design files and after looking at them now I have below takeaway for myself.

- **Always be learning :** I had no experience doing any web design nor I had a clue about the outcome of my project. Would I earn anything? However, as the saying goes, half the battle is won if you show up/get started. I made an effort to open up photoshop and start playing around with the tools. Our industry is an ever growing, always evolving, and learning is eternal. So this is an important eye opener for me. Back then I was learning, and so should now. Back then it was HTML, CSS and Php which was in demand, now we may have Machine learning, Mobile application etc. in demand and I need to always keep up with the trend.

- **Your efforts in anything will always be fruitful:**  I had learned photoshop on my own, I did not master the software but I have enough knowledge to create a transparent .png or resize an image or create a image sprite. Recently at work, I had to do some image sprite and our UX person could not match up our existing image pixels on their Apple Mac. The older image and the new additions would always be distorted. Because of my experience in photoshop back then, I could easily get new image as per our requirement. So your knowledge will always be helpful somewhere or the other. Just keep learning.

- **Every work/task can be an opportunity to learn:**  After completing my college,  I took this freelancing job where I had to do some initial design. Designing is complete opposite of what I studied however it was part of the process that I was involved in. If I had to get myself useful and get something done, I had to learn photoshop, no big deal. The point is, I cannot and should not have the attitude that I am professional with certain expertise and so I will not do this Job. I should be taking up projects and tasks that may require me learning irrespective of what the underlying domain is. In the end, it is the collective efforts from different domain/technology which actually makes a better software.
