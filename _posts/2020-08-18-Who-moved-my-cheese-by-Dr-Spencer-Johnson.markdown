# Who moved my cheese by Dr Spencer Johnson

This was a book recommended to my by a colleague at work. I have had a love hate relationship with the books earlier. There was a time when I would read a lot of books, during my school days when I finished Famous five by Enid Blyton in a day. I would discover books in the huge public library that we had and then I would hide them deep in the shelves in another section so that I could later come and rent it. That was the kind of love I had for books. Later, when I discovered sitcoms, unknowingly I lost the habit of reading. I again got back to reading but it was intermittent. 

To restart this habit, I choose to read this tiny book "Who moved my Cheese - By Dr. Spencer Johnson". This book is about how to deal with change at your work and in your life.

> Who Moved My Cheese? is a story about change that takes place in a Maze where four amusing characters look for “Cheese”

I usually avoid reading Forward, however, for this book it was exceptional. It created a curiosity.

A reunion in Chicago led to an interesting question circling around Change.  Everyone was trying to cope with the unexpected changes that were happening to them in recent years. And most admitted that they did not know a god way to handle them.

![Who moved my cheese](/assets/images/book/who-moved-my-cheese.png){:class="img-fluid w-100"}

The book proceeds to explain how to handle change in work as well as in personal life by telling a story "Who moved my cheese?"

The story is about 4 little characters who ran through a maze looking for cheese. Two of them sniff and scurry are little mice and hem and haw are little people who everyday put on their jogging pants and running shoes to go look for cheese. 

They find an excellent Cheese station and are happy to be coming back daily for that cheese. Hem and Haw get confident that the cheese would always be available, now settle down near Cheese station. Having so much confidence, they overlook any changes that appear surrounding them. One day, when they no longer find their cheese, they are in distress and deny to accept this change. While Sniff and Scurry are ready to look for new Cheese accepting the change they see at the Cheese station. 

The next day, Hem and Haw left their homes, and returned to Cheese station again, where they still expected, somehow, to find their cheese.  The situation had not changed and the little people did not know what to do. They just stood there, immobilized like two statues.

The situation was intense for Hem and Haw and they want to analyze and get to the roots of who moved their cheese. They thought they were entitled to the cheese as they were special people. 

On the other hand, sniff and scurry where already on their way looking for new cheese station. They went to the unknown maze corridors and to their surprise, found great supply of Cheese. They squealed with delight. 

In the meantime, Hem and Haw were still at the old cheese station evaluating their situation, suffering, frustrated and angry blaming each other for the situation.

Hem and Haw feared leaving their comfort zone to look for new Cheese. Everyday the little people continued to do what they had done before, go to the Cheese station and return empty handed. They denied what was happening and so found it harder to sleep, had less energy and become irritable.

Having analyzed the situation, Haw was ready to find the cheese, while Hem was still in the fears of unknown and uncertainty.  While Haw had his own fears, and the maze looked challenging, he thought to himself, "Better late that never".  Haw could find cheese here and there and sometimes loose his path to dark corridors in the maze, he reminded himself that even if it was uncomfortable situation, in reality, it was much better than staying in the cheeseless situation. 

Haw was taking control, rather than simply letting things happen to him.

While thinking of the situation, Haw began to realize that the change probably would not have taken him by surprise if he had been watching what was happening all along and if he had anticipated change. 

In the interim of his thoughts, Haw realized things and wrote it down on the wall as a lesson. Haw had certainly moved on, however the fear was still upon him, but to overcome his fear and to add a little motivation, he would write his learning on the wall.

> Smell the cheese often, so you know when it is getting old.

As Haw started running down the dark corridor, he began to smile. Haw was letting go and trusting what lay ahead of him, even though he did not know exactly what it was.

> When you move beyond your fear, you feel FREE.

Haw realized again, that what you are afraid of is never as bad as what you imagine. The fear you let build up in your mind is worse than the situation that actually exists.

> Noticing small changes Early helps you adapt to the bigger changes that are to come.

When it seemed like he had been in the maze forever, his journey landed him happily to a new cheese station. At first he was startled and at the same time surprised to find new kind of cheese. While he was figuring out whether it was real or his imagination, he saw his old friends Sniff and Scurry. 

As Haw enjoyed the new Cheese, he reflected on what he had learned.

- Sniff and Scurry kept life simple. They did not over analyze or over complicate situations. They moved on based on the situation.
- Haw was afraid of change and he was holding on to the illusion of old cheese that was no longer there.
- Haw started to change as soon as he learned to laugh at himself and at what he had been doing wrong.
- Haw learned that you could be more aware of the need to keep things simple, be flexible and move quickly.
- Adapt faster and with time.
- Biggest inhibitor to change lies within yourself.
- Go past your fear and enjoy the adventure. There is always new cheese out there to explore.
- Fear needs to be respected as it can keep you out of real danger.

After having analyzed and learned his lessons, Haw did not get comfortable at the new Cheese station and each day he would inspect the new cheese station and kept exploring the maze to avoid any unexpected surprises. 

**The discussion within the group**

Later same day after hearing the story, the group decided to meet over for drink and discuss on the story. Everyone had different stories but it all revolved around change.

To summarize, whether you are afraid or not, CHANGE is inevitable. Either accept it as early as possible before you are in the comfort zone or repent it later. In the end, we all want to be prepared for the change.
