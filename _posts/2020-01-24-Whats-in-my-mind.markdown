Starring at a blank screen is always scary and the scarier it gets when you have so much to talk about but fail to type it out. I am overwhelmed by the amount of information our thoughts make up. Even though it is not structured and unpredictably random, it is fascinating. I always wanted to have a post about my year in review for so many years. I vaguely remember that I had written about it earlier and possibly I also deleted it.

Looking at that calendar I just realized it is almost end of January. Thanos and his guntlet, looks like he snapped his finger and time accelerated. I feel I have hardly done anything worthy in the year 2019 that it should have its dedicated blog post. Or perhaps I should write about how I procrastinated, a failure blog post.
Nevertheless, today, I don't want anything structured. Why not just shout out what my brain thinks, aloud. I wish.

## Failures
- I did try. But I failed. Am I allowed to console myself? I tried keeping up with this blog. In fact I also had a weekly reminder despite my brain being wired to write as and when I have experiences to share. Something held me back? NO, I cannot blame anyone. It was always me, It will always be me.

- I also tried using Trello, trick my brain into fancying that I will do something about the tasks and ideas I have posted on the Trello board. Some I accomplished, some I procrastinated and then the enthusiasm died.

- Exercise - I failed miserably. I was proud of getting my ass to the gym, but most of 2019 I failed exponentially. The bicycle I purchased around two years back is just lying there laughing at me. I have been advocate of fitness and healthy eating for a long time. I failed.

## Plans for 2020
- Focus is what I need. Filter out my priorities. I have realized that writing all the tasks down helps me plan. Now I need to prioritize them. 
I think the best for me would be to finish the task at hand, rather doing multiple tasks simultaneously. I am not a superhuman. I wish I were, but tiny success are albeit good achievements. 
For now, my success would be to getting this post published. No distractions whatsoever. Even if I have spelling mistakes in this post I don't want to google for any corrections.

- One think I usually do to keep myself focused and avoid any external distractions is to listen to Ludovico Einaudi. I can listen to Una Mattina in an infinite loop, forever. I think it helps me with anxiety. Some listen to lyrical music, I find it more distracting. My recent love to listen to Piano music has helped me a lot.

- I recently discovered about Gatsby. I wish to learn React and move this blog from Jekyll to Gatsby. I can see that I had started this blog in Feb 2019. I took the effort of setting up Jekyll and to think of it now, I feel that it was a tiny achievement. I wish to continue updating my skills. Maintaining a blog with my thoughts will help me long term. 

- Flutter is remarkable. It is the best thing by Google and I fell in love with it. I have been following many youtube channels related to flutter, for the most of 2019, I followed youtube tutorials on flutter. Sometime back nearing end of 2019, I took the plunge of writing flutter code based on designs I found on dribble. It was an eye opener. I learned more than I could possibly imagine. Going forward I wish to fill this blog with all the amazing WOW moments I discovered when writing flutter code.

- Do at least one commit a day. The github's graph of commits is amazing. If you see the green squares filled, it feels fulfilling. I tried to keep it green for almost everyday, I failed at it for past couple of days. Nevertheless, I know what I enjoy doing and I wish to continue writing more flutter code every single day. 

## What's in my mind?
Funny as it may sound, I started writing this blog post with a different train of thoughts. And as I said in the beginning, the thoughts are random as hell. The moment you type it down on the screen, looks like your fingers filter out many of it. 

There are many things in my mind, but the best part is, today I started my day with a tiny achievement, checking off "write a blog post".

Let's be productive.

Adios.
